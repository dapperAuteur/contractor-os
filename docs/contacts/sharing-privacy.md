# Sharing Contacts — Privacy Controls

## Overview

When you share a contact with another Work.WitUS user, you control exactly which fields they receive. This lets you share useful information (like a company name and email) while keeping sensitive details (like personal phone numbers and your private notes) completely private.

## How to Share a Contact

1. Go to **Contacts** and open the contact you want to share
2. Click the **Share** button (top-right)
3. Enter the **username** of the person you're sharing with
4. Optionally add a **message** explaining why you're sharing
5. Use the **"What to share" checkboxes** to select which fields to include:

| Field | What it shares |
|-------|---------------|
| **Name** | Always shared (cannot be unchecked) |
| **Company** | Company name, city, state, country |
| **Job Title** | Contact's job title |
| **Email** | All email addresses |
| **Phone** | All phone numbers |
| **Notes** | Your notes about this contact |
| **Addresses** | Physical/mailing addresses |
| **Tags** | Company, role, skill, group tags |
| **Website** | Website URL |
| **Pay Portal** | Paycheck portal URL and company ID |

6. Click **Share**

## What Happens

- The recipient receives a **pending share notification**
- When they **accept**, a copy of the contact is created in their account with **only the fields you checked**
- Fields you unchecked are **never sent** — they remain completely private
- The recipient's copy is **independent** — their edits don't affect your original

## Examples

### Sharing for a job referral
- Check: Name, Company, Email, Job Title
- Uncheck: Phone, Notes, Addresses, Tags, Website, Pay Portal
- Result: Recipient gets the business essentials for reaching out

### Sharing a full crew contact
- Check everything
- Result: Recipient gets a complete copy including pay portal access

### Sharing with limited info
- Check: Name, Company only
- Result: Recipient just knows who works where — no personal details

## Privacy Guarantee

- **Unchecked fields are never transmitted.** They don't appear in the share record, the API response, or the recipient's copy.
- **Your original contact is unchanged.** Sharing creates a copy; it doesn't give access to your contact record.
- **Recipients can't see what you didn't share.** They won't even know which fields you chose to withhold.
- **Each share is independent.** You can share the same contact with different people with different field selections.
